🌱 Our Story

We Believe Every Life Deserves Dignity

Helping Hearts was born from a simple belief — that compassion, when organized and transparent, can transform society. Here's who we are and why we do this.

How It Started

A Simple Idea That Changed Lives

In 2020, a group of young Pakistanis watched their community struggle through the economic aftermath of the pandemic — families without food, children missing school, patients unable to afford basic treatment.

They didn't wait for someone else to fix it. They created Helping Hearts — a platform to bridge generous donors with people in real need, with radical transparency at its core.

"If we could show donors exactly where every rupee goes, they'd give more — and people in need would get more. So that's what we built."

Today, Helping Hearts has raised over PKR 42 million and helped more than 15,000 people across 12 cities in Pakistan.

What Drives Us

Our Core Values

These aren't just words on a wall. They're the principles we apply to every campaign, every rupee, every interaction.

🔍

Radical Transparency

Every donation is tracked and reported with field photos and financial breakdowns. You always know exactly where your money goes and what it accomplished.

⚡

Speed of Impact

In a crisis, every hour counts. We deploy 98% of funds to beneficiaries within 48–72 hours — because lives depend on how fast we move.

🤝

Community First

We partner with verified local NGOs, hospitals, and schools embedded in communities — people who know exactly what's needed and how to deliver it.

❤️

Human Dignity

Every person we help has a name, a story, and full dignity. We treat each beneficiary with the care and respect they deserve — not as a statistic.

🛡️

Accountability

We hold ourselves to the highest standard. Our finances are independently audited and every campaign outcome is documented and published for donors.

🌱

Long-Term Thinking

Beyond emergency relief, we invest in education and health programs that build sustainable futures — breaking cycles of poverty, not just temporarily relieving them.
